Why are my listings ranked lower than other listings similar to me?

Rankings combine several signals—not only price and location. Completeness of your listing, how recently it was updated, response time to inquiries, guest feedback, and policy compliance can all influence placement. Similar listings may rank higher if they are more complete, more actively managed, or better match what searchers are selecting in filters.

Why am I being recommended more expensive listings when there are similar ones at a lower price?

Recommendations are not sorted by price alone. The system may weigh availability, match to your stated preferences, historical booking success, distance, amenities, and quality or trust signals. In some cases a higher-priced option may look like a better overall match for the criteria being used—even when a cheaper listing appears superficially similar.

Why are the pricing suggestions always lower than what I expected?

Suggested prices are typically modeled from recent market transactions, seasonality, and comparable listings. Models often lean conservative to reflect observable demand rather than an aspirational rate, which can feel “low” if you’re comparing to peak-season peaks or informal word-of-mouth comps.

Suggestions are guidance, not a guarantee of what you will earn; your final price remains your decision. More granular explanations of the inputs used for your property will be linked from this portal as those reports become available.
